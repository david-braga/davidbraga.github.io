Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made and the consequences of the action you took.

When I started working as a receptionist in a Backpacker in Queenstown, life was awesome because I was always meeting different people from all over the world, doing bungee jumps, skydiving, white water rafting, etc, all for free, besides living in one of the most beautiful cities in the world.

I wasn't making enough money, though, to do anything else other than pay rent and food. One day, on the way back home after work, I decided to take the "scenic route" and instead of walking on the street I took the track that goes along Lake Wakatipu. It was almost night time and I didn't see anyone else all the way. When I was getting close home I found a wallet on the ground with a lot of money on it (enough to hold me on for a couple of months). I took the wallet and won't lie I thought about keeping it without saying anything, but I didn't. By the time I got home all I could think of was how the person who had lost it was feeling and what that money could be for so I walked all the way back to town and handed in the wallet at the police station. Walking back home again, all I could feel and think of was peace of mind.
