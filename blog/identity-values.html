IDENTITY - VALUES - STRENGTHS

After self-reflecting a lot, speaking to family members and friends I can say I'm a reserved person, who values the family, friendship, integrity and perseverence. There's one particular situation that really marked me.

When I started working as a receptionist in a Backpacker in Queenstown, life was awesome because I was always meeting different people from all over the world, doing bungee jumps, skydiving, white water rafting, etc, all for free, besides living in one of the most beautiful cities in the world. I wasn't making enough money, though, to do anything else other than pay rent and food. One day, on the way back home after work, I decided to take the "scenic route" and instead of walking on the street I took the track that goes along Lake Wakatipu. It was almost night time and I didn't see anyone else all the way. When I was getting close home I found a wallet on the ground with a lot of money on it (enough to hold me on for a couple of months). I took the wallet and won't lie I thought about keeping it without saying anything, but I didn't. By the time I got home all I could think of was how the person who had lost it was feeling and what that money could be for so I walked all the way back to town and handed in the wallet at the police station. Walking back home again, all I could feel and think of was peace of mind.

I considere myself a perseverant person so when I want something I give everything to get it, doesn't matter how hard it is. I would say that's the main strength I've got to keep on going during this course even though I can easily get distracted when reading or studying,
